A Framework for Serendipity

Heuristics / Unexpected Interaction

The first heuristic, UNEXPECTED INTERACTION, describes systems that explore alternative methods of design that break with conventions and explore the divergent and, consequentially serendipitous potential that systems potential.

Defamiliarisation

Through the Defamiliarisation, or estrangement, of information or interface, designers are able to make the experience unfamiliar, while reclaiming the interactors attention.

Examples of Defamiliarisation
  • Argeïphontes Lyre. Rabelais, Akira.
  • Papers, Please. 2013. 3909 LLC.

Speculative Interactions

Through Speculative Interactions, systems abandon functionality in order instil in the interactor a particular emotion, one that is not, necessarily, related to the system’s proposed goal.

Examples of Speculative Interactions
  • Gravity Clock. 2010. Jörg Piringer
  • Unfair Mario. n/a.
  • Serendipitor. 2010. Shepard, Mark.

Errors, Glitches, and Interference

While through Errors, Glitches, and Interference, planned or otherwise, the systems’s expected functionality is abandoned and allows for unpredictability and unexpectedness in the interaction.

Examples of Errors, Glitches, and Interference
  • Homage to New York. 2012. Tinguely, Jean.
  • Hack ‘n’ Slash. 2014. Double Fine Productions.
